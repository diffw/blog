## More About Me

A Christian, a designer, the husband of one woman, and the father of three children.

I first encountered the internet in college in 2001 and became deeply immersed in web design and Flash animation, witnessing the most open era of the Chinese internet. In 2009, I shifted to app design and became one of the earliest mobile internet designers in China.

Born in Changsha, Hunan, I lived in Hangzhou for seven years and Shanghai for nine years. After immigrating to the United States, I spent two years in Los Angeles and now reside in Dallas, Texas.

## What Am I Doing Now?

Currently, I'm working on several design projects that explore the intersection of digital art and user experience. I'm particularly interested in how minimalism can create more meaningful interactions in our increasingly complex digital world.

I'm also spending time developing a new design system that focuses on accessibility and inclusivity, ensuring that beautiful design can be experienced by everyone.

Outside of work, I'm actively involved in my local church community in Dallas, where I contribute to various design and communication projects. I'm also enjoying the process of documenting my design journey and sharing insights with the design community.

I'm always open to interesting collaborations and conversations about design, faith, and the creative process.
